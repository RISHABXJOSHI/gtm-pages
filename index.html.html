3 campaign ideas I would test for Hypergen

These are not final strategies. They are examples of how I think through sourcing, enrichment, and outbound angle.

1
Clutch → Clay

Pull agency lists from Clutch, enrich on Clay to get verified email IDs

Source
Clutch.co — filter by category, location, company size, or review count to get a targeted agency list.
Enrichment
Feed the list into Clay and run waterfall enrichment to find verified contact email IDs for the right personas.
Why it works
Clutch companies are actively listed and visible — they want clients. That intent signal alone makes them warmer to cold outreach than a generic database pull.
Email angle
“Saw your reviews on Clutch — looks like your delivery is strong. Most agencies at your stage are working on making pipeline more predictable.”
2
GetLatka → Clay ICP filter

Source SaaS companies from GetLatka, score ICP fit in Clay before enriching

Source
GetLatka — pull SaaS companies filtered by revenue range, employee count, or growth signals relevant to the campaign objective.
Enrichment
Push into Clay and run a custom Clay command to score whether each company fits the ICP. Only accounts that pass move on to waterfall enrichment — no wasted lookups on bad-fit companies.
Why it works
GetLatka has self-reported SaaS data most providers don’t carry. The Clay ICP gate keeps the list clean and keeps enrichment costs low by only spending credits on qualified accounts.
Email angle
“Pulled your numbers from GetLatka — looks like you’re at a stage where outbound usually starts mattering more than inbound alone.”
3
Directory scrape → public emails

Scrape franchise or niche directories for publicly listed contact emails

Source
Niche directories like Franchimp — where companies are listed with their own contact info on the page (help@, contact@, info@, hello@).
Enrichment
Run a web scraper through each company’s website to extract publicly visible email addresses directly — no third-party data provider needed.
Why it works
Private emails from waterfall enrichment are often inactive or buried under cold email volume. Publicly listed emails are checked regularly and placed there intentionally — making them more likely to get opened and answered, especially when the sender is treating them as a warm channel rather than a spray-and-pray one.
Email angle
“Found your contact email on [directory]. Most people in your space are hard to reach — figured a direct note made more sense than going through a gatekeeper.”
